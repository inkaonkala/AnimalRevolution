	==================================================
	1. HIGH CONCEPT
	==================================================

Animal Revolution is a cozy-dark satire management and exploration game inspired by George Orwell’s Animal Farm and modern factory farming systems.

The player begins as one of two revolutionary dogs living in a human-owned apartment building. After overthrowing their owner, the dogs begin transforming the building into an animal-run society. As the sanctuary expands, animals take over different systems within the tower:
- Rodents grow vegetables
- Cats manage meat production
- Fish maintain water systems
- Spiders provide medicine
- Parrots lure in more humans
- Dogs govern leadership, trade, and propaganda

The player gradually shifts from obedient revolutionary to independent decision-maker as the ruling dog becomes increasingly lazy and privileged.

The game blends:
- Top/down 2D exploration
- Floor-by-floor management
- Day/night cycle
- Automation progression
- Moral and ideological choices

Tone:
Cute, cozy pixel world with deeply satirical systems.

	==================================================
	19. FARMING SYSTEM
	==================================================

CORE PURPOSE:
Farming is the first major renewable survival system and the foundation of early-game food security.

EARLY GAME:
- Player manually plants
- Player manually waters
- Limited seeds
- Limited planting space
- Hamsters assist later
- Fish eventually improve automation

==================================================
19A. ROOFTOP FARM LAYOUT
==================================================

STARTING ROOFTOP:
- 4 planting pots total (not all necessarily available immediately)
- Recommended early unlock pacing:
  - Week 1 Start: 1–2 pots active
  - Later: Additional pots unlocked
- Each pot:
  - 4x4 planting grid (16 spaces)
  - 1 plant per square

FULL ROOFTOP POTENTIAL:
- 4 pots = 64 total plants

DESIGN NOTE:
Starting with fewer active plots reduces early repetitive watering and preserves cozy pacing.

==================================================
19B. CROP TYPES
==================================================

CARROT:
- Growth Time: 4 days
- Yield:
  - 1 carrot
  - 1 seed
- Purpose:
  - Reliable starter crop
  - Basic rodent food

---

POTATO:
- Growth Time: 6 days
- Yield:
  - 2 potatoes
  - Random chance for 2 seeds
- Purpose:
  - Bulk food
  - More stable crop economy

---

CUCUMBER:
- Growth Time: 10 days initial
- Yield:
  - 5 cucumbers
  - Random chance for 1 seed
- Special:
  - Vine crop
  - Only needs planting once
- Purpose:
  - Long-term investment crop
  - High output

---

SUNFLOWER:
- Growth Time: 8 days
- Yield:
  - 6 seeds
  - 1 flower
- Purpose:
  - Seeds, Bird food	
  - Flower, can be sold for high price
  - Possible therapy / morale bonus

==================================================
19C. WATERING RULES
==================================================

PLAYER WATERING:
- Water bottle required
- One bottle waters 6 plants
- Bottle filled from sink/tap
- Plants must be watered before player sleeps

IF WATERED:
- Growth progresses by 1 day

IF NOT WATERED:
- Plant does not grow that day

DESIGN PHILOSOPHY:
No crop death from missing one day.
Failure is delay, not punishment.

==================================================
19D. HAMSTER FARM SUPPORT
==================================================

HAMSTER WATERING:
One hamster automatically waters plants during night.

BASE OUTPUT:
- Happy hamster: 3 plants
- Sad hamster: 2 plants

HAMSTER NEEDS:
- Vegetables
- Shelter

PURPOSE:
Reduce player burden while preserving farming involvement.

==================================================
19E. FISH TANK WATERING SYNERGY
==================================================

Fish improve hamster watering efficiency.

FISH ROLE:
- Maintain water systems
- Improve irrigation support
- Require hose expansion and tank maintenance

FISH TANK MAINTENANCE:
- Player collects additional hoses
- maintain tank
- Better tank = happier fish
- Neglected tank = sad fish

WATERING COMBINATIONS:

Happy Fish + Happy Hamster:
- 8 plants watered/night

One Happy + One Sad:
- 6 plants watered/night

Sad Fish + Sad Hamster:
- 4 plants watered/night

DESIGN PURPOSE:
Creates interconnected species systems:
Fish health + Hamster happiness = Farming efficiency

	==================================================
	20. INVENTORY & ITEM MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
	==================================================

CORE PURPOSE:
Inventory supports exploration, farming, construction, feeding, selling, and resource transport.

The system should feel flexible and cozy, not punishing.

==================================================
20A. PLAYER INVENTORY
==================================================

STARTING INVENTORY:
- Player starts with 5 inventory slots.
- Items are stackable.
- Default stack limit: 20.
- Stack limits should be coded per item so values can be changed during playtesting.

DESIGN NOTE:
Exact stack sizes will be balanced later through testing.

==================================================
20B. ACTIVE ITEM SLOT
==================================================

Player has one special “active / in-use” item slot.

The active item is used when the player interacts with the world.

Possible active items:
- Seeds -> plant
- Water bottle -> water plants
- Claws / attack symbol -> attack humans or destroy walls

DESIGN PURPOSE:
Keeps interaction simple and readable.
Inspired by tool/item use in cozy farming games.

==================================================
20C. ITEM MOVEMENT
==================================================

Player can:
- Move items inside inventory
- Stack matching items
- Split stacks if needed
- Place items into storage
- Take items from storage
- Carry products to shop
- Move food to feeding systems

==================================================
20D. STORAGE SYSTEM
==================================================

EARLY STORAGE:
- Storage inside home apartment.

MID GAME STORAGE:
- Floor 4 can become a larger storage area.

FUTURE STORAGE:
- Species/floor-specific storage may be added later.

DESIGN PURPOSE:
Storage supports expansion without forcing the player to carry everything manually forever.

==================================================
20E. FEEDING SYSTEM
==================================================

ROOFTOP FEEDING BOX:
- Player puts animal food into the rooftop feeding box.
- Animals eat from it during the night.
- Food inside is converted into leftovers.

LEFTOVER TUBE:
- Leftovers are placed into a feeding tube next to the box.
- The tube sends leftovers to the human factory.

SYSTEM LOOP:
Crops -> Feeding Box -> Animals eat -> Leftovers -> Tube -> Humans

DESIGN PURPOSE:
Creates a clear resource chain between farming, animal care, and human farming.

==================================================
20F. BALANCING NOTES
==================================================

DEFAULT STACK LIMIT:
- Start with 20 for most items.

POSSIBLE FUTURE ITEM-SPECIFIC LIMITS:
- Seeds: 20
- Vegetables: 20
- Scrap wood: 20
- Plastic: 20
- Tape: 10
- Hot glue: 10
- Hose: 1
- Water bottle: 1
- Pillows: 5

These values are placeholders and should be adjusted after playtesting.

==================================================
20G. DESIGN RISKS
==================================================

RISK:
Too much carrying becomes tedious.

SOLUTION:
- Add storage early.
- Add larger storage later.
- Let animals automate some transport in later chapters.
- Keep stack sizes generous.

### Object System Design (Prototype Architecture)

To keep development modular, scalable, and manageable, interactable world elements are divided into functional object categories rather than creating unique scenes for every single item.

#### 1. Pickup Items

Pickup Items are objects the player can collect and store in inventory.

Examples:

* Seeds
* Water bottles
* Food
* Keys
* Crafting materials

These use a shared base scene (`PickupItem`) with editable variables such as:

* `item_id`
* `amount`

This allows one flexible system to represent many collectible objects without building separate scenes for each variation.

---

#### 2. Plant Spots

Plant Spots are interactive farming spaces that change through multiple growth stages over time.

Possible stages:

* Empty soil
* Dug soil
* Seeded
* Growing
* Harvestable
* Dead

A Plant Spot tracks:

* Current stage
* Crop type
* Water state
* Growth progress

This keeps farming logic centralized while allowing multiple crop types to use the same base system.

---

#### 3. Structures

Structures are larger persistent world objects that provide functionality, progression, or environmental interaction.

Examples:

* Elevator
* Beds
* Shops
* Therapy rooms
* Factories

These are designed as separate systems because they often involve unique mechanics, navigation, or story progression.

---

### Design Philosophy

This system prioritizes:

* Reusability
* Easy expansion
* Cleaner code structure
* Faster prototyping
* Simpler inventory and save systems

By separating objects by purpose rather than appearance, the project avoids unnecessary scene duplication and supports future expansion more efficiently. This modular approach aligns with Godot’s scene-based workflow and supports long-term project scalability.
